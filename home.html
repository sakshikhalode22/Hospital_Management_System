BLOOD BANK

Blood is a specialized bodily fluid that delivers necessary substances to the bodyâ€™s cells such as nutrients and oxygen, and transports waste products away from those same cells. Blood comprises more than 8% of the body weight of a healthy individual. On an average, every adult person has about 5 to 6 liters of blood. The major component of blood is a fluid called plasma in which are suspended cellular elements. These are Red Blood Cells or RBCs, White Blood Cells or WBCs and platelets.
